Cookie Policy


What is Cookie Compliance?
From May 2011 a new privacy directive came into effect across the EU. The resulting new law requires that websites ask visitors for consent to use web cookies. Cookies are an extremely common technology that helps websites remember information about you such as your preferences as you move between pages or even to track the number of visitors to our website. The overreaching aim for the directive is to inform you that we are committed to respecting the privacy rights of every visitor to our website and to reassure you that we do not use cookies to store personal information about you. As part of this, we aim to provide you full transparency as to which cookies are being set and why they are necessary.

What Are Cookies?
Cookies are small encrypted text files dropped on your computer when you visit websites via your browsers. Our use of cookies is to primarily help enhance your user experience and improve the efficiency of our website. In order to make purchases on our website and for an optimal user experience, you will need to enable cookies on your computer, tablet or mobile phone.

How do you turn off cookies?
Changing your browser’s settings so that it will not accept cookies may restrict the functionality of our website. However, if you still wish to disable cookies, please visit the following section:How to turn off cookies on your browser?, or contact us at info@villamentonehotel.co.uk for further information.
Please note that, if you do not wish to enable cookies (or if you disable cookies), you will still be able to browse our website and use it for research purposes.

How We Use Cookies?
We do use cookies to collect contextual information about your visit to the site to enhance the functionality of site and the user experience.

We Use Cookies to:
Collect information to help us understand browsing habits such as preferred language and contextual information that also help you navigate the site easily. This helps us improve the overall browsing experience on thistle.com
Collect information that will allow us to tailor advertising to make it relevant for you, based on previous interactions with our website
Remember information about your visit to the site

Types of Cookies that we use on our website
Functional Cookies Functional cookies are cookies that help the thistle.com website function properly such as remembering your selections as you move between pages on thistle.com. These cookies do not temper or access any personal data. They are temporary and last as long as your visit on our site.
Personalization Cookies We at thistle.com use cookies for personalization based on the user preferences like language attributes or login information provided by you when you choose the 'Remember Me' option. These cookies are used only for user experience and do not store any personal information. They do remember certain attributes such as language you made in the booking in beyond your current visit so that the next time you return to our site, we display our information in the same language.
Third Party Cookies When you visit thistle.com you may notice some cookies do not belong to us. These are called Third Party cookies and they dropped by other parties when you visit our website.
Analytics- We use Analytics cookies to allow us to count page visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. We also use a software called TagMan to help us see which advertisements you click on and interact with. The data taken is not confidential or interchangeable.
Remarketing- We have relationships with selected partners who may also set cookies during your visit to be used for remarketing. This helps us show you different products and services based on what is relevant to you and what you have expressed an interest in. We feel this saves you valuable time from having to search for the relevant information.

If you do not use such platforms then our site will not place these cookies. We don't control the setting of these cookies, so we suggest you check the third-party websites for more information about their cookies and how to manage them.
